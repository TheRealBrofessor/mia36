# Behind the Screen

> **It's never too late to learn cybersecurity.**

A structured, beginner-friendly cybersecurity learning roadmap by **TheRealBrofessor**.

Designed for people who feel like technology moved too fast and left them behind. No prior experience needed. Just patience, curiosity, and the willingness to learn one concept at a time.

---

| Phase | Title | Core Analogy |

## How This Works

Each chapter builds on the one before it. By Chapter 7, you'll have booted Linux, used the terminal, understood networking, watched traffic, navigated safely, installed software, and built your own virtual lab. That's a complete beginner-to-practitioner journey.

## Design Principles

1. **Every chapter has an analogy** — unfamiliar tech mapped to everyday life
2. **Cumulative skill building** — each chapter reuses ALL prior skills + adds one new thing
3. **Hardware context woven in** — no standalone hardware chapter; hardware introduced where relevant
4. **Anxiety-aware** — every chapter starts by validating the reader's nervousness
5. **Muscle memory** — by the end, the reader has a complete workflow

## Who This Is For

- People who feel left behind by modern technology
- Career changers curious about cybersecurity
- Anyone who tried to learn tech and got overwhelmed
- People who think cybersecurity is "not for them"

## Getting Started

Start with [Phase 1: Digital Confidence](chapters/chapter-1-2/) — you'll boot Ubuntu from a USB drive and prove to yourself that you can try something new without breaking anything.
